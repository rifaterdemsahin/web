To estimate how much you might be able to borrow from a bank in the UK for a 15-year fixed-rate mortgage, several factors come into play, including your income, expenses, debts, and the interest rate of the mortgage. Generally, lenders use a multiple of your annual income (often around 4.5 times your annual income), but this can vary widely depending on the lender's criteria and your personal financial situation.Here's a simplified formula to get a rough estimate:[ \text{Maximum Loan Amount} = \text{Annual Income} \times \text{Income Multiple} - \text{Existing Debts} ]Keep in mind, this formula doesn't take into account the interest rate or the term of the mortgage, which will affect your monthly repayments. The interest rate, in particular, can significantly impact how much you can afford to borrow because it determines the cost of the loan.For a more accurate figure, you would need to consider the interest rate and use a mortgage calculator that takes into account your monthly affordability. This would involve knowing the exact annual percentage rate (APR) of the mortgage offer.
