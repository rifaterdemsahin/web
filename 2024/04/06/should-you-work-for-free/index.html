Should you work for free ?

Whether or not you should work for free to build skills in video production depends on your individual circumstances, goals, and the specific opportunity presented. Here are some considerations to help you make an informed decision:

  • Skill Development: If you're passionate about learning video production and believe that working on weddings with your friend will provide valuable hands-on experience, then it could be worth considering. Weddings can be complex events to film, and they can offer a range of challenges that can help you develop your skills.

  • Portfolio Building: Building a portfolio is crucial in the creative industries like videography. Working for free on a few weddings can provide you with material to showcase your work to potential clients or employers in the future.

  • Time and Commitment: Consider how much time you can realistically dedicate to this opportunity. If you have other commitments like a job or studies, ensure that working on weddings won't overly strain your schedule.

  • Relationship with the Friend: Evaluate your relationship with your friend and ensure that working together won't strain your friendship. Clear communication about expectations, responsibilities, and boundaries is essential to avoid potential conflicts.

  • Future Opportunities: While working for free initially can be a good way to gain experience, it's important to assess whether there are opportunities for paid work in the future. If your friend's wedding business grows and you're still providing valuable services, you may want to negotiate fair compensation down the line.

  • Value of Your Work: Even if you're working for free, it's important to recognize the value of your contributions. Make sure your friend understands that while you're willing to help out without compensation initially, your time and skills have value.

Ultimately, the decision to work for free to build skills in video production should align with your goals and circumstances. If you believe that the experience gained from working on weddings with your friend will be beneficial to your skill development and career aspirations, then it could be a worthwhile opportunity. Just make sure to approach it thoughtfully and with clear expectations.
