A Family Day Out at Chesterton Sports Club via Uber

Planning a family day out can be as thrilling as it is cumbersome, especially when trying to keep things fun yet budget-friendly. Recently, our family decided to visit Chesterton Sports Club, a popular destination for families looking to engage in various sports and recreational activities. Here’s a breakdown of our experience, from the transportation to the cost of entry, and the overall enjoyment of the day.

Getting There: Choosing Uber

For transportation, we opted for Uber. The convenience of not having to navigate or park was a big plus, and with the fare being quite reasonable, it seemed like the best choice. The ride cost us £8 one way, which I found to be quite economical for the comfort and hassle-free experience it provided.

Parkside pool is walking distance

Entry Costs and First Impressions

Upon arrival, we were greeted by the welcoming staff of Chesterton Sports Club. Entry fees were £6 per person, amounting to £24 for our family of four. This entry fee granted us access to a variety of facilities including swimming pools, tennis courts, and other sporting areas.

The club itself was well-maintained, with plenty of activities to keep each of us engaged. Whether it was the kids splashing around in the pool or us adults trying our hand at tennis, the vibrant atmosphere made it an enjoyable experience for all ages.

Activities and Facilities

One of the highlights of Chesterton Sports Club is the wide range of activities available. From swimming and tennis to a fully equipped gym and running tracks, there was something for everyone. The kids particularly enjoyed the swimming pool which featured a few water slides and play areas.

For adults, the tennis courts were in excellent condition, and we even had the chance to join a friendly match with some fellow visitors. The gym was another plus point, equipped with modern facilities that catered to all our fitness needs.

Dining Options

No family outing is complete without some good food, and the sports club did not disappoint. The onsite café offered a variety of snacks and meals, perfect for refueling after a few hours of physical activity. The prices were reasonable, and the food quality was commendable.

Parkside pool has pizza and coffee

Conclusion: Worth the Trip?

Overall, our trip to Chesterton Sports Club was a fantastic outing. The total cost, including Uber rides both ways and the entry fees, came to £56. This felt like good value considering the range of activities and facilities available. It was a day well-spent, where we could enjoy quality family time without breaking the bank.

If you’re looking for a fun, active day out with the family, Chesterton Sports Club is certainly a place worth considering. Just remember to check the availability of facilities beforehand and maybe even book your spots in advance during peak times!

As we continue to explore more family-friendly venues around our area, stay tuned for more updates and reviews on where to go and what to expect. Happy adventuring!
