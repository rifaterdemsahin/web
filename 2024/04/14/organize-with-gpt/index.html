The error message you're encountering typically occurs in environments where smart card authentication is used, especially in Windows systems. This message indicates a problem with how the domain information is structured or presented in the smart card logon certificate, making it difficult for a non-domain-joined computer (a computer that is not part of a larger network of computers managed centrally) to recognize and authenticate the user properly. Here's what you can do to address this issue:

  • Contact System Administrator: As the error message suggests, the first and most straightforward step is to contact your system administrator. This issue often involves configuration settings that are not accessible to the average user.

  • Certificate Configuration: The administrator should check the smart card's logon certificate, particularly the Distinguished Name (DN) field. The DN needs to include specific domain information that the system can use to verify the user's credentials. It's possible the certificate was not properly issued or lacks necessary domain information.

  • Update Certificate Template: If the certificate template used to issue smart card certificates is missing necessary information, the administrator may need to update the template to include these details. This change typically involves adjusting the template in the Certificate Authority that issues these certificates.

  • Reissue Certificate: If the existing certificate is not configured correctly, it might be necessary to reissue a new certificate with the proper settings and information in the DN field.

  • Test Configuration: After adjustments are made, testing with a non-domain-joined computer is crucial to ensure the problem is resolved. This can help verify that the domain information is now correctly included and recognized during the authentication process.

  • Review Group Policy Settings: For domain-joined computers, it's also a good idea to review Group Policy settings related to smart card login to ensure they are correctly configured and enforced.

  • Documentation and Support: Sometimes, specific scenarios or configurations might require looking into the vendor’s documentation or seeking direct support from the software or hardware provider of the smart card systems.

Addressing this issue typically requires administrative access and a good understanding of the domain and certificate configurations, so coordinating with IT support is essential.
