In Canva, creating animations with timed orders for objects involves using layers and the animation features provided in the editor. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how you can time the order of objects in your Canva design:

  • Create Your Design:

  • Start by adding all the objects (like text, images, shapes) to your design. Each object can be adjusted on its own layer.

  • Layering Your Objects:

  • To manage layers, select an object and right-click on it. You will see options to bring the object forward or send it backward. This helps in positioning which object is on top of another.

  • Properly layering your objects will be crucial when you start adding animations, as animations are applied per object.

  • Applying Animations:

  • Select the object you want to animate. Click on the animate icon in the top toolbar. This will show you a variety of animation styles like Breathe, Fade, Pan, etc.

  • Apply an animation style to each object as per your design requirement.

  • Timing the Animations:

  • As of now, Canva doesn’t allow precise control over the exact timing (like seconds) directly through its interface. However, you can control the order of the animations.

  • The animation timing is generally set by the order of how you apply the animations to each object. Objects animated first will play their animation before objects that are animated after.

  • Preview and Adjust:

  • Use the play button in the editor to preview your animations. This helps you see how your animated elements come together.

  • If the order isn't right or the effect isn't as expected, you can go back and reapply the animations in a different sequence or adjust the layering of the objects.

  • Export Your Animated Design:

  • Once satisfied with the timing and order, export your design. Canva allows you to download your animated designs as GIFs or videos.

Remember, while Canva provides basic animation capabilities, the control over timing and advanced animation effects is somewhat limited compared to more specialized animation software. If you need more precise control over timing or more complex animations, you might need to consider software like Adobe After Effects.

If you're looking for alternative online tools to create animations, particularly with more control over timing and effects than Canva offers, there are several good options available. Here are some popular tools that cater to various levels of expertise and specific needs:

  • Adobe Creative Cloud Express (formerly Adobe Spark):

  • This is a more intuitive tool compared to Adobe’s professional suite, ideal for beginners and intermediate users. It allows for simple animations and can be used for social media graphics, short video clips, and more.

  • Crello:

  • Similar to Canva, Crello offers a drag-and-drop interface with a variety of templates. It supports animations and is very user-friendly, making it suitable for creating animated social media posts, ads, and other digital content.

  • Animaker:

  • Animaker is a robust tool that caters to creating animated videos with a focus on user-friendly features. It provides features like character animations, voiceovers, and a wide range of animation effects and timelines for precise control.

  • Vyond (formerly GoAnimate):

  • Vyond is great for creating animated informational videos, like explainer videos or instructional materials. It offers a range of characters and scenarios that can be customized and animated according to specific needs.

  • Moovly:

  • Moovly is another strong alternative that offers extensive libraries of stock videos, sounds, and images, along with the ability to upload your own assets. Its editor is timeline-based, which allows for detailed control over the timing of animations.

  • Powtoon:

  • Powtoon is designed to create video presentations and animated videos that are engaging and visually appealing. It’s especially popular in business and education sectors for its ease of use and professional-quality output.

  • Biteable:

  • Biteable is a simple-to-use online tool for creating animations and is particularly good for quick, stylish video ads and social media videos with a variety of templates and stock footage.

  • Renderforest:

  • Renderforest offers video creation, logos, website, and graphic design. It’s useful for producing music visualizations, professional intros, and animated explainer videos with a significant degree of customization.

Each of these tools offers a unique set of features, so the best one for you will depend on your specific needs, the complexity of the animations you want to create, and your budget.
