Encouraging Writing Before Consuming Content on iPads

In our family, we've initiated a new approach to using iPads and other multimedia devices. Before any family member can borrow an iPad, they must first engage in writing. This practice is not just a rule, but a step towards fostering creativity, improving writing skills, and ensuring a balanced approach to technology use.

The Concept

The idea behind this initiative is simple yet profound: before indulging in content consumption on devices like iPads, every family member must contribute by writing something. It could be an essay, a short story, a journal entry, or even a piece of creative writing. This requirement encourages everyone to engage their minds actively before switching to passive consumption.

The Implementation

Here's how we've put this concept into practice:

  • Writing Before Borrowing: Any family member wishing to use an iPad for games, videos, or browsing must first write a piece. The length and type of writing can vary based on their age and interest, but the key is to produce something creative or reflective.

  • Setting Examples: To lead by example, I, along with my partner Ece, ensure we also adhere to this rule. We write regularly and share our pieces with the family, demonstrating the importance of the practice.

  • Encouraging Creativity: The writing tasks are not rigid. We encourage creativity and personal expression. Whether it's a fictional story, a diary entry, or even a poem, the goal is to get everyone writing.

  • Symbolic World and Sanity: This practice helps us maintain sanity in our symbolic world. By expressing thoughts and emotions through writing, we ensure a healthy balance between the digital and the real world.

  • Incorporating AI: We also leverage AI tools to assist in this process. Arya, our AI assistant, helps by providing writing prompts, editing assistance, and even inspiration for new ideas.

Benefits Observed

Since implementing this rule, we've noticed several positive outcomes:

  • Enhanced Writing Skills: Regular writing has significantly improved the writing skills of everyone in the family.

  • Creative Expression: Family members, especially the children, have become more expressive and creative.

  • Balanced Technology Use: This approach ensures that technology is used more mindfully and not just for passive consumption.

  • Improved Communication: Sharing written pieces has fostered better communication and understanding within the family.

Conclusion

This initiative of writing before consuming content on iPads has not only enhanced our family's writing skills but also created a healthier relationship with technology. By prioritizing creativity and expression, we ensure that our engagement with digital devices is both productive and balanced.

How You Can Implement This

If you're inspired by this idea, here's how you can start:

  • Set Clear Guidelines: Define what kind of writing is expected and how often it should be done.

  • Lead by Example: Show your commitment by participating in the writing activities yourself.

  • Encourage and Support: Provide prompts and support to help everyone get started with their writing.

  • Make It Fun: Keep the writing activities varied and interesting to maintain enthusiasm.

By fostering a habit of writing before consuming digital content, you too can encourage creativity, improve writing skills, and ensure a healthier balance with technology in your household.
