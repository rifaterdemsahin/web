budgetting

Setting a budget for Google AdWords depends on several factors, including your goals, competition, and the estimated cost per click (CPC) in your niche. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you determine an appropriate budget:

  • Research CPC: Find out the average cost per click for keywords related to your course. For example, "Selenium course," "C# testing course," or "DevOps automated testing."

  • Estimate Conversion Rate: Determine an estimated conversion rate (the percentage of clicks that convert into sales). A conservative estimate might be around 1-2%.

  • Calculate Desired Sales: Decide how many courses you aim to sell each month.

  • Calculate Ad Spend: Use the formula:
    [
    \text{Budget} = \left( \frac{\text{Desired Sales}}{\text{Conversion Rate}} \right) \times \text{CPC}
    ]

Example Calculation

  • Average CPC: $1.50 (assuming from your research)

  • Conversion Rate: 2% (0.02)

  • Desired Sales per Month: 50 courses

[
\text{Budget} = \left( \frac{50}{0.02} \right) \times 1.50 = 2500 \times 1.50 = \$3750 \text{ per month}
]

Adjusting for Smaller Budgets

If $3750 per month is too high, you can adjust by:

  • Reducing the number of desired sales.

  • Targeting less competitive keywords with lower CPC.

  • Starting with a test budget (e.g., $500 per month) to gather data and adjust as needed.

Starting Point

  • Test Budget: Start with $500 per month to gather initial data.

  • Daily Budget: Approximately $16-17 per day.

This initial budget allows you to test the waters, analyze performance, and adjust based on actual data.

Monitoring and Adjusting

  • Monitor performance regularly.

  • Optimize your ads and keywords based on what converts best.

  • Increase the budget as you see positive returns and scale your campaign.

Conclusion

Starting with a $500 test budget and scaling up based on performance is a safe approach. Always monitor and adjust based on the data to ensure you are maximizing your return on investment.
