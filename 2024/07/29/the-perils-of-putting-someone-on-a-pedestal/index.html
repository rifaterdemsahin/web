The Perils of Putting Someone on a Pedestal

In our lives, it’s natural to admire certain people. Whether it’s a celebrity, a romantic partner, a friend, or a mentor, admiration can be a positive force, driving us to aspire and grow. However, there’s a fine line between admiration and idealization, and crossing that line can lead to putting someone on a pedestal. This can have significant implications for both the person being idolized and the one doing the idolizing.

What Does "Putting on a Pedestal" Mean?

"Putting on a pedestal" refers to elevating someone to an exalted status, viewing them as nearly perfect and infallible. This often involves ignoring or downplaying their flaws and shortcomings, leading to an unrealistic perception of who they truly are. It’s akin to seeing them through rose-colored glasses, where their positive traits are magnified and their negative traits are minimized or entirely overlooked.

Why Do We Put People on Pedestals?

There are various reasons why we might place someone on a pedestal:

  • Admiration: Deep respect or admiration for someone's talents, qualities, or achievements can lead us to elevate them.

  • Insecurity: Feeling inadequate or lacking in certain areas can make us idolize those who possess qualities we desire.

  • Love and Infatuation: In romantic relationships, infatuation can blind us to our partner’s imperfections, leading to idealization.

  • Lack of Information: Sometimes, we simply don't know someone well enough to see their flaws, leading to an incomplete and idealized view.

The Dangers of Idealization

Putting someone on a pedestal can have several negative consequences:

  • Unrealistic Expectations: When we idealize someone, we expect them to live up to our lofty image of them. This can create pressure and stress for the person being idolized and lead to disappointment when they inevitably fall short.

  • Stunted Relationships: True, healthy relationships are built on mutual respect and understanding. Idealization can prevent genuine connection and communication, as one person is seen as superior.

  • Self-Esteem Issues: For the person doing the idolizing, constantly comparing oneself to an idealized figure can damage self-esteem and create feelings of inadequacy.

  • Neglecting Reality: Ignoring someone's flaws can lead to poor decision-making, especially in romantic relationships. It can prevent us from addressing issues that need attention.

Finding a Balanced Perspective

While it’s natural to admire and respect people, maintaining a balanced perspective is crucial. Here are some tips to avoid putting someone on a pedestal:

  • Acknowledge Flaws: Recognize that everyone has flaws and shortcomings. Acknowledging this can help you see people more realistically.

  • Focus on Yourself: Instead of comparing yourself to others, focus on your growth and development. Admiration should inspire you, not make you feel inferior.

  • Open Communication: In relationships, open and honest communication is key. Discussing both strengths and weaknesses helps build a more realistic and balanced connection.

  • Cultivate Self-Worth: Build your self-esteem by recognizing your own worth and accomplishments. Understand that everyone, including you, has unique qualities that deserve appreciation.

Conclusion

Admiration and respect are positive feelings that can inspire and motivate us. However, it's important to keep our admiration grounded in reality. By recognizing the humanity in everyone, we can form healthier, more balanced relationships and avoid the pitfalls of putting someone on a pedestal. Embrace the imperfections in yourself and others, and you'll find a more authentic and fulfilling connection with the world around you.
