Comparing the RX Vega M GL mobile GPU and the RTX 6900 XT AMD desktop GPU involves looking at several key specifications and performance metrics. Here's a breakdown of the main differences:

RX Vega M GL Mobile GPU:

Architecture:

  • Vega

Manufacturing Process:

  • 14nm

Compute Units:

  • 20 CUs (1280 Stream Processors)

Clock Speeds:

  • Base Clock: 931 MHz

  • Boost Clock: 1011 MHz

Memory:

  • 4 GB HBM2

  • Memory Bandwidth: 179.2 GB/s

TDP:

  • 65W

Target Market:

  • Mobile/laptop applications, integrated into Intel processors like the Intel Kaby Lake-G series.

Performance:

  • Suitable for medium to high settings in older games at 1080p

  • Limited capabilities for modern AAA games at high settings

Radeon RX 6900 XT Desktop GPU:

Architecture:

  • RDNA 2

Manufacturing Process:

  • 7nm

Compute Units:

  • 80 CUs (5120 Stream Processors)

Clock Speeds:

  • Base Clock: 1825 MHz

  • Game Clock: 2015 MHz

  • Boost Clock: Up to 2250 MHz

Memory:

  • 16 GB GDDR6

  • Memory Bandwidth: 512 GB/s

TDP:

  • 300W

Target Market:

  • High-end desktop applications, gaming, and professional workloads.

Performance:

  • Excellent performance for 4K gaming and VR

  • Capable of handling modern AAA titles at the highest settings

  • Strong performance in professional applications requiring high compute power

Comparison:

  • Architecture & Process:

  • RX Vega M GL uses the older Vega architecture and 14nm process.

  • RX 6900 XT utilizes the newer RDNA 2 architecture and 7nm process, offering better efficiency and performance.

  • Compute Units & Stream Processors:

  • RX Vega M GL has 20 CUs (1280 SPs), which is significantly lower than the RX 6900 XT's 80 CUs (5120 SPs).

  • Clock Speeds:

  • The RX Vega M GL has much lower clock speeds compared to the RX 6900 XT, reflecting its lower performance tier.

  • Memory:

  • The RX Vega M GL comes with 4 GB HBM2 memory, while the RX 6900 XT is equipped with 16 GB GDDR6 memory, providing much higher capacity and bandwidth.

  • TDP:

  • RX Vega M GL has a low TDP of 65W, suitable for mobile platforms.

  • RX 6900 XT has a high TDP of 300W, fitting its high-performance desktop role.

  • Performance:

  • The RX Vega M GL is designed for medium to high settings in older games at 1080p resolution and is more suited for integrated laptop applications.

  • The RX 6900 XT offers top-tier performance, ideal for 4K gaming, VR, and demanding professional workloads, delivering much higher performance across the board.

In summary, the RX Vega M GL is a mobile GPU optimized for efficiency and integrated laptop use, while the RX 6900 XT is a high-end desktop GPU designed for maximum performance. The RX 6900 XT far outperforms the RX Vega M GL in all aspects, from compute power and memory capacity to overall gaming and professional application performance.
