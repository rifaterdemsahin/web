Bulk deleting apps on an iPhone can be done in a few different ways, depending on the iOS version you're using and whether you want to delete multiple apps at once from the home screen or through the Settings app.

Bulk deleting apps on an iPhone can be done in a few different ways, depending on the iOS version you're using and whether you want to delete multiple apps at once from the home screen or through the Settings app. Here’s how you can do it:

Method 1: Deleting Apps from the Home Screen

  • Press and Hold on an App Icon: On the home screen, press and hold any app icon until all the icons start to jiggle, and a minus (-) sign or an "X" appears in the corner of each app.

  • Select Apps to Delete: Tap the minus (-) sign or "X" on each app you want to delete. A confirmation dialog will appear asking if you want to delete the app.

  • Confirm Deletion: Tap "Delete App" in the confirmation dialog to remove the app. Repeat this for each app you wish to delete.

  • Finish: When you're done, tap "Done" or press the Home button (if your iPhone has one) to stop the jiggling.

Method 2: Deleting Apps from the Settings App

  • Open the Settings App: Tap on the "Settings" app on your iPhone.

  • Navigate to General: Go to "General" > "iPhone Storage."

  • Review Installed Apps: You'll see a list of all installed apps on your device, along with the amount of storage each one uses.

  • Select an App to Delete: Tap on the app you want to delete, then tap "Delete App." Confirm the deletion when prompted.

  • Repeat for Each App: Repeat this process for each app you want to remove.

Method 3: Using Offload Unused Apps Feature (Automatically)

This method doesn't delete apps, but it can help clear space by removing apps you don't frequently use while keeping their data.

  • Open Settings: Tap on the "Settings" app.

  • Go to App Store: Tap on "App Store."

  • Enable Offload Unused Apps: Toggle on "Offload Unused Apps." This will automatically offload apps you don't use regularly but will keep their documents and data.

Method 4: Using iTunes (On a Computer, older iTunes versions only)

If you're using a computer with an older version of iTunes (before iTunes 12.7), you can manage and delete apps in bulk from your computer:

  • Connect iPhone to Computer: Connect your iPhone to your computer and open iTunes.

  • Access iPhone Summary: Click on the iPhone icon in iTunes, then go to the "Apps" tab.

  • Manage Apps: You'll see your home screen layout in iTunes. You can delete apps by clicking the "Remove" button next to each app in the list.

  • Sync Changes: Once you're done, click "Apply" to sync your changes to your iPhone.

These methods will allow you to manage and delete apps from your iPhone efficiently. The first two methods are the most straightforward for most users.
