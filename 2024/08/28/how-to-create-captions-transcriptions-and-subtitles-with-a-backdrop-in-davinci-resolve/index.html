How to Create Captions, Transcriptions, and Subtitles with a Backdrop in DaVinci Resolve

DaVinci Resolve is a powerful video editing software that offers a wide range of tools for professionals and enthusiasts alike. One of its lesser-known yet incredibly useful features is the ability to create captions, transcriptions, and subtitles with ease. In this blog post, we'll walk you through the steps to add captions and subtitles to your videos and how to enhance them with a backdrop for better readability.

Step 1: Preparing Your Project

Before diving into the captioning and transcription process, make sure your project is set up correctly:

  • Open DaVinci Resolve and load your project. If you’re starting fresh, create a new project and import your video footage by going to the Media Pool tab and dragging your files into the timeline.

  • Ensure your video is edited to your liking. Captions and subtitles should typically be added after the bulk of your editing is done.

Step 2: Adding Captions or Subtitles

DaVinci Resolve allows you to add captions (used for accessibility) and subtitles (often used for translating dialogue). Here’s how you can do it:

  • Go to the Edit Page:

  • This is where you’ll do most of your editing work, including adding text elements like captions and subtitles.

  • Access the Caption Track:

  • Right-click on the timeline and choose Add Subtitle Track. This creates a dedicated track for your captions or subtitles.

  • Insert Captions or Subtitles:

  • Navigate to where you want your caption or subtitle to appear. Right-click on the subtitle track and select Add New Subtitle. A new subtitle block will appear on your subtitle track.

  • Edit the Text:

  • Click on the subtitle block to open the inspector on the top right corner. Here, you can type in the text of your caption or subtitle. Adjust the duration of the subtitle block to match the timing of the dialogue in your video.

Step 3: Transcribing Audio to Text

To streamline the captioning process, DaVinci Resolve also offers tools for transcribing audio to text. This is especially useful for videos with a lot of dialogue.

  • Select Your Audio Clip:

  • Click on the audio clip in the timeline that you want to transcribe.

  • Use the Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) Feature:

  • Go to Timeline > Transcribe Audio. DaVinci Resolve will use its built-in speech recognition to automatically generate a transcript of the audio clip. This might take some time, depending on the length and clarity of the audio.

  • Review and Edit the Transcription:

  • Once the transcription is complete, review the text for accuracy. ASR isn’t perfect, and there may be mistakes depending on the audio quality and the clarity of speech. Edit the text directly in the transcription panel.

  • Convert Transcription to Subtitles:

  • After refining your transcription, you can convert it directly into subtitles by selecting Convert to Subtitles. DaVinci Resolve will automatically break the transcription into subtitle blocks based on the dialogue's natural pauses.

Step 4: Adding a Backdrop to Your Subtitles

To enhance the readability of your subtitles or captions, especially over complex backgrounds, adding a backdrop is a great idea.

  • Select Your Subtitle Block:

  • Click on a subtitle block in the timeline to open the inspector panel on the right.

  • Access Subtitle Settings:

  • In the inspector panel, scroll down to find the Background settings under the subtitle or caption settings.

  • Enable Background:

  • Toggle the background option to “on.” You can adjust the color and opacity of the backdrop to suit your video’s style. A common choice is a semi-transparent black background, which ensures the text remains readable without blocking too much of the underlying footage.

  • Adjust the Background Shape and Size:

  • Use the Padding and Corner Radius sliders to adjust the backdrop’s size and shape. You can also add a soft edge to the background to create a subtle gradient effect.

  • Apply Changes:

  • Repeat these steps for each subtitle block or use the copy-paste attributes feature to apply the same backdrop settings to all subtitle blocks for consistency.

Step 5: Exporting Your Video with Captions and Subtitles

Once you’ve added all your captions, subtitles, and backdrops, it’s time to export your video.

  • Switch to the Deliver Page:

  • Click on the Deliver page at the bottom of DaVinci Resolve.

  • Choose Your Export Settings:

  • Select the desired format and quality settings for your export. Ensure the Subtitle Settings are set to Export Subtitles or Burn into Video, depending on your preference.

  • Export Your Video:

  • Click on the Add to Render Queue button and then Start Render. DaVinci Resolve will process your video and include your captions or subtitles as per your settings.

Conclusion

DaVinci Resolve offers robust tools for adding captions, transcriptions, and subtitles to your videos. With its user-friendly interface and powerful features like automatic speech recognition and customizable backdrops, you can ensure your content is accessible and visually appealing. By following the steps outlined in this guide, you'll be able to enhance your videos with professional-quality captions and subtitles, making them more engaging for all viewers.

Happy editing!

By following these steps, you can effectively use DaVinci Resolve to add captions, transcriptions, and subtitles with backdrops to your videos, enhancing both accessibility and visual clarity.
