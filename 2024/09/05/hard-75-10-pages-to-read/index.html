10 Pages to Read During the Hard 75 Challenge

The Hard 75 Challenge has become a popular mental and physical toughness test. Designed by entrepreneur Andy Frisella, the program lasts 75 days and requires strict discipline in various areas of life, including fitness, nutrition, and mental growth. One key element of the challenge is reading at least 10 pages of a non-fiction book every day. But with so many books out there, where should you start? Here are 10 pages that can make a difference on your Hard 75 journey.

1. "Atomic Habits" by James Clear

Why? Building better habits is essential for success on Hard 75, and Atomic Habits provides science-backed strategies for creating and sticking to routines that last. Clear's tips can help make daily habits like working out and eating healthy feel automatic.

2. "Can't Hurt Me" by David Goggins

Why? Can’t Hurt Me is a memoir of extreme discipline and resilience. Goggins' relentless mindset offers invaluable lessons on mental toughness, which you’ll need during Hard 75’s most grueling days.

3. "The Power of Now" by Eckhart Tolle

Why? Hard 75 isn't just a physical challenge—it's also about mastering your mind. Tolle’s book teaches you to be present, which can be particularly helpful when you’re pushing through tough workouts or resisting cravings.

4. "Grit: The Power of Passion and Perseverance" by Angela Duckworth

Why? Duckworth’s research on grit is highly relevant for anyone taking on a 75-day challenge. Learn how to stay passionate and persevere even when the road gets tough.

5. "The 5 AM Club" by Robin Sharma

Why? One of the best ways to manage your time during Hard 75 is by starting your day early. Sharma's book outlines the benefits of waking up before the world does, giving you more time for workouts, meal prep, and reading.

6. "Mindset: The New Psychology of Success" by Carol S. Dweck

Why? Growth mindset is key to surviving the Hard 75 Challenge. Dweck’s book explains how shifting from a fixed mindset to a growth mindset can help you improve, even when you feel like quitting.

7. "Extreme Ownership" by Jocko Willink and Leif Babin

Why? Hard 75 requires you to take full responsibility for your actions every day. Willink and Babin’s book, based on their Navy SEAL experiences, drives home the value of accountability and leadership in life and fitness.

8. "The Obstacle Is the Way" by Ryan Holiday

Why? Inspired by Stoic philosophy, Holiday’s book reminds you that obstacles are opportunities in disguise. The Hard 75 Challenge will present numerous obstacles, and The Obstacle Is the Way will help you tackle them with the right mindset.

9. "You Are a Badass" by Jen Sincero

Why? Sincero’s book is packed with motivational advice for improving your life. If you ever need a mental boost during Hard 75, her words will remind you of your inner strength and potential.

10. "The 4-Hour Body" by Tim Ferriss

Why? For fitness and nutrition hacks, Ferriss’ book is full of practical advice. Although Hard 75 requires strict adherence to a nutrition plan, The 4-Hour Body may provide inspiration for ways to improve your physical progress even further.

Conclusion:

During the Hard 75 Challenge, reading 10 pages a day is not just a mental break—it’s a powerful tool for growth. By choosing books that focus on discipline, mental strength, and habit-building, you’ll get more than just knowledge; you’ll gain strategies that can help you stay committed to the challenge.
