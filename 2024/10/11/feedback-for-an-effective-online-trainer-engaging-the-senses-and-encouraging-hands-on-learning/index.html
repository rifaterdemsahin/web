Feedback for an Effective Online Trainer: Engaging the Senses and Encouraging Hands-On Learning

In today’s online training environment, creating an engaging and interactive experience is key to effective learning. As I observed in a recent session, there are a few crucial areas that can significantly enhance the training process for both the instructor and the students. Here is my feedback for trainers looking to deliver impactful, hands-on sessions.

1. Engage Multiple Senses – Avoid Static Screens

One of the most important aspects of online learning is keeping the audience engaged. A static screen with minimal changes can quickly lead to disengagement, especially when paired with a single person talking for an extended period. It’s essential to involve not just the ears (listening), but also the eyes (visual elements) and hands (active participation). This multi-sensory engagement helps maintain focus and ensures better retention of information.

To combat the problem of a static screen, trainers should include dynamic visual elements, such as slides that change frequently, live demonstrations, and interactive diagrams. Additionally, tools like polls, quizzes, or shared whiteboards can help keep the eyes and hands involved. This variety prevents the session from becoming a one-way monologue, which can cause learners to tune out.

2. Proper Preparation of Visual Materials

Another critical point is the readability and structure of your presentation. A font size of at least 30 ensures that everyone can clearly see the content, regardless of the device they are using. Large, clear fonts reduce eye strain and improve comprehension.

Moreover, preparing a Kanban board or a clear guide before the session allows for better navigation through the training material. This structure provides a roadmap for learners, offering them direction rather than placing the entire focus on the instructor. A guided process creates a collaborative environment, where learners are partners in the discovery, rather than passive receivers of information.

3. Hands-On Learning is Crucial

Perhaps the most important feedback is ensuring that the students are actively participating throughout the session. It’s not enough to simply tell and show; students must do and apply the concepts themselves. The training should follow a cycle of telling (explaining), showing (demonstrating), doing (students performing the tasks), and applying (students practicing in real time).

This hands-on approach helps to solidify the learning. As trainers, we should be able to see students actively participating during the online session. Encourage them to share their screens, troubleshoot issues, and demonstrate their understanding in real time. This method not only reinforces the material but also ensures that learners walk away with practical experience, rather than just theoretical knowledge.

Conclusion

For online training to be effective, it must be engaging, structured, and hands-on. By involving multiple senses, preparing clear and readable visual materials, and ensuring students actively participate, trainers can significantly improve the learning experience. This feedback is intended to help trainers shift away from one-way communication and towards a more interactive, learner-centered approach that fosters real understanding and application of the material.
