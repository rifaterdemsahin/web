Why You Need to Take a Kolbe Conation Test

Understanding your natural instincts is crucial for optimizing how you work and achieving your best results. This is where the Kolbe Conation Test comes into play. Conation is your natural way of doing things, your instinctual drive, and it goes beyond mere behavior—it's about what comes naturally to you.

What is the Kolbe Conation Test?

The Kolbe Conation Test, often referred to as the Kolbe A Index, measures your conative strengths—how you take action when you are free to be yourself. It reveals your instinctive method of operation (MO) in four key areas:

  • Fact Finder - How you gather and share information.

  • Follow Thru - How you organize and design.

  • Quick Start - How you deal with risk and uncertainty.

  • Implementer - How you handle space and tangibles.

My Kolbe Test Results

For example, my Kolbe Test results show that I am high in Fact Finder and Quick Start, and adaptable in Follow Thru and Implementer:

  • Fact Finder: I excel in simplifying complex information, distilling it to its essence, making it easier to understand. I prefer clear, zero-to-one explanations.

  • Follow Thru: I thrive on multitasking and can juggle multiple responsibilities simultaneously.

  • Quick Start: I am very high in quick starting, meaning I can initiate projects and adapt to changes rapidly without much hesitation.

  • Implementer: I restore systems and ensure they are operational, focusing on both abstract concepts and hands-on work.

Why Take the Kolbe Test?

  • Maximize Your Strengths: Understanding your natural strengths allows you to focus on tasks that align with your instincts. For example, as a high Quick Start, I know I can dive into projects and adapt on the fly without extensive preparation.

  • Improve Your Efficiency: Knowing how you naturally operate can help you streamline your workflow. If you're a high Fact Finder, you might excel at thorough research before starting a project. If you're a high Quick Start, jumping straight into action might be your best approach.

  • Reduce the Need for External Motivation: When you work in a way that aligns with your natural instincts, you won't need as much external motivation. You'll find yourself more naturally engaged and driven.

  • Better Team Collaboration: Understanding your conative strengths can also improve how you work with others. You'll be better equipped to communicate your needs and understand how to complement your team members' strengths.

How to Use Your Kolbe Results

  • Quick Start: If you're like me and high in Quick Start, focus on initiating projects and adapting as you go. Don't spend too much time in the planning phase; get started and refine along the way.

  • Fact Finder: If you score high in Fact Finder, take the time to gather detailed information and ensure you fully understand a project before diving in.

  • Follow Thru: If Follow Thru is your strength, create detailed plans and stick to them, ensuring every step is accounted for.

  • Implementer: If you're strong in Implementer, focus on hands-on tasks and physical projects where you can see tangible results.

Conclusion

Taking the Kolbe Conation Test can provide valuable insights into your natural working style. It helps you align your tasks with your instincts, improving efficiency and reducing the need for external motivation. Whether you're a Quick Start ready to dive into new projects or a Fact Finder who excels in thorough research, understanding your conative strengths can significantly enhance your productivity and job satisfaction.

So, if you haven't taken your Kolbe Conation Test yet, I highly recommend it. It can be a game-changer in understanding how you work best and leveraging your natural strengths to achieve your goals effortlessly.
