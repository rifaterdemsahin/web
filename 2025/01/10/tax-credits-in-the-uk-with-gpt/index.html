Tax credits in the Uk with Gpt

Thanks for clarifying! If you’re earning £450 per day on an IR35 contract, your situation would look like this:

Income Calculation

• Gross Income:

• £450/day × 20 working days = £9,000/month.

• Post-IR35 Deductions:

• Inside IR35, your income is taxed like an employee’s salary. Assuming typical deductions for PAYE, NI, and Employer’s NI, your net monthly income might be around £5,400–£6,000, depending on tax codes and expenses.

• Combined Household Income:

• Your net: ~£5,400–£6,000/month.

• Your wife’s net: ~£1,400/month (approximate).

• Total: £6,800–£7,400/month (net).

Child Tax Credits or Universal Credit

Given your combined income, it is unlikely you would qualify for Child Tax Credits or Universal Credit because:

• Universal Credit has a household income limit; for most families, your income is too high to qualify.

• Child Tax Credits thresholds are also unlikely to cover your income bracket.

Other Child Support Options

You might still benefit from other forms of childcare support:

  1. Tax-Free Childcare:

• You can get up to £500 every 3 months (up to £2,000 a year per child) as a government top-up on what you pay into a childcare account.

  1. 30 Hours Free Childcare:

• If your youngest child is aged 3–4 and both parents work, you may qualify for 30 hours free childcare.

  1. Claiming Child Benefit:

• Even with high income, you can claim Child Benefit (approx. £24/week for the first child and £15.90/week for additional children).

• However, if one parent earns over £50,000, the High-Income Child Benefit Charge will reduce the benefit, and over £60,000, it’s effectively canceled out.
