0Storage Management

Managing 36TB of NAS storage, while syncing only 5TB with Google and handling an additional 10TB on Apple, requires a clear and structured approach. Here's a step-by-step plan tailored to your needs:

1. Analyze and Categorize Your Data

  • Categorize Data by Type:

Media (movies, TV shows, music)

  • Documents

  • Photos

  • Backups

  • Miscellaneous files

  • Identify Storage Priorities:

Decide which files need frequent access (sync with Google or Apple) versus those that can remain on the NAS for archival purposes.

2. Optimize NAS Storage

  • Delete Redundant Files: Use tools like Synology’s Storage Analyzer to find and remove duplicate files or unused data.

  • Compress Files: Archive infrequently used files with compression tools.

  • Tier Storage: Keep high-priority or frequently accessed files on faster NAS drives, and archival files on slower drives.

3. Plan Google Sync (5TB Limit)

  • Selective Sync Strategy:

Use Synology Drive or Google Drive to sync only essential files.

  • Prioritize syncing work documents, critical backups, and personal photos.

  • Not all is usefull to sync

  • Exclude Media Files:

Avoid syncing large media files like movies and TV shows with Google; keep them on the NAS for Plex.

  • Google Workspace Alternative: Upgrade to a Google Workspace plan with increased storage if needed.

  • Increase storage can it be an option i am at 5 tb

4. Organize Apple Storage (10TB Limit)

  • Sync Specific Content:

Use Apple’s storage for photo libraries, family backups, and key documents.

  • Offload Media: Avoid syncing large Plex libraries or low-priority content.

  • Enable Optimize Storage on Apple Devices:

Use Apple’s built-in storage optimization to free up space automatically.

5. Manage Plex Media Library

  • Storage Strategy for Plex:

Store all media files (movies, TV shows, music) exclusively on the NAS.

  • Use Plex’s Optimize Feature to create lower-resolution versions of media for faster streaming and reduced storage usage.

  • Regular Cleanup:

Remove old, unwatched media or use Plex’s “Watched” status to identify files that can be archived or deleted.

6. Set Up Backups

  • Primary NAS Backup:

Use another Synology NAS, external drives, or cloud storage for incremental backups of critical data.

  • Cloud Backup Priorities:

Ensure backups of essential documents and irreplaceable data (photos, personal files) to Google or Apple.

  • Archive Non-Essential Data:

Archive older, less important files to cold storage (e.g., external drives or offline HDDs).

7. Monitor and Maintain

  • Use Synology Tools:

Regularly monitor storage usage with Storage Manager and set up alerts for low space.

  • Implement Quotas:

Set user quotas or folder-level quotas to prevent uncontrolled growth in storage usage.

  • Automated Cleanup:

Schedule regular deletion or archiving of logs, temp files, and old data.

8. Plan for Growth

  • Expand NAS Storage:

If you foresee continued storage needs, consider expanding your NAS with larger drives or adding a second unit.

  • Third-Party Cloud Storage:

Explore other cloud options (e.g., Dropbox, Amazon S3, Backblaze) for affordable storage expansion.

This approach ensures efficient use of your 36TB NAS, while managing the 5TB Google and 10TB Apple limits. Let me know if you'd like help implementing specific steps or choosing tools!
