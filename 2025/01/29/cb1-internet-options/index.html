If you’re looking for fast internet access in the UK, here are some of the best options:

Public Wi-Fi Options

  1. Libraries & Universities

• Most public libraries and university libraries offer free Wi-Fi.

• Cambridge Central Library and University of Cambridge provide Wi-Fi through CambWifi, eduroam, or UniOfCam-Guest networks.

  1. Cafés & Co-Working Spaces

• Many cafés (e.g., Starbucks, Costa Coffee, Pret A Manger) offer free Wi-Fi.

• Co-working spaces like WeWork, Regus, and Impact Hub often provide high-speed fibre internet.

  1. Public Wi-Fi Networks

• BT Wi-Fi Hotspots (for BT customers)

• O2 Wi-Fi (free in certain public places)

• Virgin Media Wi-Fi (available in London Underground stations)

• The Cloud (found in hotels, restaurants, and shopping centres)

Mobile & Personal Options

  1. 5G Mobile Data

• 5G networks from EE, Vodafone, Three, and O2 offer fast speeds (200 Mbps+ in major cities).

• A 5G mobile hotspot device or a phone with tethering can give you internet anywhere.

  1. Portable Wi-Fi (MiFi)

• Devices like EE 5GEE Router, Vodafone GigaCube, and Three 4G Hub provide fast, portable internet.

  1. Home Broadband

• Full Fibre (FTTP) broadband from providers like BT, Virgin Media, Hyperoptic, and Community Fibre can reach 1 Gbps+ speeds.

• Check if gigabit fibre is available in your area for the fastest speeds.
