Replacing the battery in your Dyson V6 cordless vacuum is a straightforward process that doesn’t require soldering. Dyson designs their batteries to be user-replaceable, secured with screws for easy removal and installation. Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you through the process:

Tools and Materials Needed:

• Phillips-head screwdriver

• Replacement Dyson V6 battery

Safety Precautions:

• Ensure the vacuum is turned off and unplugged before beginning.

• Work in a clean, well-lit area.

• Handle all components with care to avoid damage.

Step-by-Step Battery Replacement:

  1. Prepare the Vacuum:

• Unplug the vacuum from the charger.

• Place the vacuum on a soft surface to prevent scratches.

  1. Remove the Dust Bin:

• Press and hold the red button to release the bin.

• Detach the bin from the main body of the vacuum.

  1. Locate and Remove Screws:

• Identify the three screws securing the battery: one on the handle and two on the underside of the vacuum.

• Use the Phillips-head screwdriver to remove these screws.

  1. Detach the Old Battery:

• Gently pull the battery away from the vacuum body.

• Set the old battery aside for proper disposal.

  1. Install the New Battery:

• Align the new battery with the slot in the vacuum.

• Slide it in until it clicks into place.

• Secure the new battery by tightening the three screws.

  1. Reassemble the Vacuum:

• Reattach the dust bin to the main body.

• Ensure all components are securely in place.

  1. Test the Vacuum:

• Charge the new battery fully before use.

• Turn on the vacuum to confirm it’s operating correctly.

Proper Disposal of the Old Battery:

Do not dispose of the old battery in regular household waste. Instead, take it to a local recycling center or a designated battery disposal point. Many electronics retailers and municipal waste facilities offer battery recycling services.
