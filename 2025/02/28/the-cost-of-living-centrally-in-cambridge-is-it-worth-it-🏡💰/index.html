The Cost of Living Centrally in Cambridge: Is It Worth It? 🏡💰

From April 2019 to February 2025, you would have paid:

1395 × 71 = £99,045

Cambridge is a city known for its stunning architecture, world-class university, and vibrant atmosphere. But living in the heart of the city comes at a price. If you’ve been paying £1,395 per month since April 2019, you’ve spent nearly £100,000 on rent! 😲 That’s a serious investment—so, is it worth it? Let’s break it down.

✅ Pros of Living Centrally in Cambridge

  1. Everything at Your Doorstep 🚶‍♂️

Living centrally means short commutes—whether it’s to work, university, or social events. You can walk or cycle everywhere, avoiding traffic and transport costs.

  1. Access to Culture & Entertainment 🎭

Cambridge offers museums, theatres, markets, and events all year round. Living centrally means you can easily enjoy the best of what the city has to offer.

  1. No Need for a Car 🚲

With Cambridge’s cycling culture and excellent public transport, you save on car costs like petrol, insurance, and parking.

  1. Great Social Scene 🍻

Living in the city means you’re always close to pubs, cafes, restaurants, and nightlife—ideal for meeting friends and networking.

  1. Beautiful Surroundings 🌳

The River Cam, historic colleges, and green spaces are just minutes away. It’s a great place for walks, picnics, or a relaxing coffee by the water.

❌ Cons of Living Centrally in Cambridge

  1. Expensive Rent 💸

As your £1,395 per month shows, rent in Cambridge is high. Living centrally often means paying a premium for location.

No increase so it is mitigated

  1. Small Living Spaces 📏

For the price, you might not get a large home or garden. Flats in the city centre can be compact and pricey per square meter.

3 bedrooms so mitigated

  1. Tourist Crowds 🚶‍♀️🚶‍♂️🚶‍♂️

Cambridge is a major tourist attraction. Living centrally means dealing with busy streets, especially in summer and on weekends.

Cambridge relatively small

  1. Limited Parking 🚗

If you do own a car, parking is a nightmare—expensive permits and limited spaces make it a hassle.

Dedicated parking

  1. Noise Levels 🔊

The city centre is lively, but that also means late-night noise from bars, traffic, and tourists. If you value peace and quiet, this might be a downside.

Isolated by trees

🎯 Final Verdict: Is It Worth It?

If you love convenience, culture, and an active lifestyle, then living centrally in Cambridge is a fantastic choice. However, if you prioritize space, affordability, and a quieter environment, moving slightly further out might be the smarter move.
