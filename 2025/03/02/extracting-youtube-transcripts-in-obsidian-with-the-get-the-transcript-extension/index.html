Extracting YouTube Transcripts in Obsidian with the 'YTranscript' Extension

Obsidian is a powerful knowledge management tool, and with the right extensions, it can become even more versatile. One such extension,YTranscript allows users to quickly extract YouTube transcripts and store them as notes in Obsidian. This can be particularly useful for researchers, students, and content creators who want to capture key insights from videos without manually transcribing them. In this post, we'll walk through how to use this extension effectively.

Why Use the 'YTranscript' Extension?

If you frequently watch YouTube videos for learning, you might want to save transcripts for later reference. This extension automates the process, enabling you to:

  • Extract transcripts directly from YouTube videos.

  • Store them as structured notes within Obsidian.

  • Enhance your second brain by linking transcripts to related notes.

  • Improve searchability and retrieval of video content.

How to Use the 'Get the Transcript' Extension in Obsidian

Step 1: Paste the YouTube URL

Copy the URL of the YouTube video you want to extract the transcript from and paste it into the designated field within the extension.

Example:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6VOwlzpZ2qw

Step 2: Open the Side Panel

Once you've pasted the URL, click on it. This will trigger the extension to open the side panel where the transcript retrieval options are displayed.

Step 3: Access More Options

Click on the three-dot menu within the side panel. This will provide additional actions and settings related to the transcript extraction.

Step 4: Save the Transcript as a Document

Select the option to Get the Notes as a Document. The transcript will then be extracted and formatted as a document within your Obsidian vault.

How This Can Benefit Your Workflow

  • Efficient Note-Taking: Instead of manually writing down key points, you can instantly generate transcripts and highlight important sections.

  • Better Organization: By linking video transcripts to other notes in your vault, you create a web of interconnected knowledge.

  • Faster Content Creation: If you’re a content creator, this extension can help you quickly reference and repurpose video content for blogs, scripts, or reports.

Final Thoughts

The YTranscript extension for Obsidian is a game-changer for anyone who relies on YouTube as a learning or research tool. By automating transcript extraction, it saves time and makes knowledge management smoother. If you haven't tried it yet, give it a go and take your Obsidian workflow to the next level!

​To integrate transcription capabilities into Obsidian, you can utilize the YTranscript plugin, which allows you to extract transcripts from YouTube videos directly into your notes. Here's how to install and use it:

  • Access Community Plugins:

Open Obsidian.

  • Navigate to Settings (click the gear icon in the lower-left corner).

  • Select Community Plugins from the sidebar.

  • Enable Community Plugins:

If not already enabled, toggle the Safe Mode off to allow third-party plugin installations.

  • Install YTranscript:

Click on Browse to open the plugin marketplace.

  • In the search bar, type YTranscript.

  • Locate the YTranscript plugin by Łukasz Strzępek and click Install.

  • Activate the Plugin:

After installation, click Enable to activate the YTranscript plugin.

Using YTranscript:

  • In your note, paste the YouTube video URL.

  • Select the URL text.

  • Press Ctrl+P (or Cmd+P on Mac) to open the command palette.

  • Type YTranscript: Get YouTube transcript from selected URL and select it.

  • The transcript will appear in a side panel with timestamps.

  • You can adjust the frequency of timestamps in the plugin settings.

  • Clicking on a timestamp will open the video at that specific point.

  • You can drag and drop lines from the transcript into your notes as needed.
