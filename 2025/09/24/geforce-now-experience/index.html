🎮 GeForce NOW Testing with NVIDIA Shield: So Many Manual Steps! 🛠️

Steps

💡 What I Wanted to Achieve:

A seamless gaming experience using GeForce NOW with my NVIDIA Shield. But what I got instead was a series of manual steps that tested my patience. Here’s the journey:

🔍 Setting Up: Not as Easy as It Should Be

While Steam and Epic Games were ready to roll, integrating them with GeForce NOW was another story. There were multiple manual steps—account linking, app installations, and ensuring everything synced up correctly. It felt like a mini-project! 🤯

🛠️ Troubleshooting: Steam Didn’t Start

On Windows, Steam wouldn’t start after installation. This was frustrating, especially since GeForce NOW requires your library to be linked. After some trial and error, I managed to get it working, but this wasn’t exactly "plug-and-play." 😤

🚗 Fortnite + Rocket Cars: Good Timing to Play

Once the setup was done, I hopped into Fortnite to test things out. The rocket car mode was a blast! 🚀 My home internet held up, making the experience enjoyable. Still, I couldn't shake off how many steps it took to get here.

🎮 Streaming Roblox for My 8-Year-Old

GeForce NOW doesn’t have everything—my 8-year-old prefers Roblox, which isn’t fully supported. So, they stuck to their usual mix of Roblox and YouTube gaming videos. Maybe a future update will bridge this gap? 🤔

🤔 Is It Worth It?

While the gaming quality on GeForce NOW is fantastic once you’re in, the manual setup process leaves a lot to be desired. I’m hoping for a more streamlined approach in future updates—fewer hoops to jump through would make this service even better. 🌟
